## Project Goal

PushAssist was built between February 2024 and May 2024. The goal was to
prototype a system that reduces manual pushing effort while keeping movement
smooth, predictable, and easy to control.

The project focused on assisted mobility: not replacing the person controlling
the motion, but making the pushing action feel lighter and more manageable.

## What I Built

- Mechanical frame for the assist mechanism
- Motor and controller setup for variable assist
- Basic control logic for throttle input and response smoothing
- Power wiring and early safety checks
- Short test workflow for starting, stopping, and handling behavior

## Development Notes

The most important part of this project was control feel. Assist that reacts too
quickly can feel unsafe, while assist that reacts too slowly does not feel
useful. I worked on finding a balance between available torque, stability, and
predictable response.

This project also pushed me to think more carefully about user interaction. A
mobility system has to feel trustworthy, especially when it starts moving under
power.

## Results

The prototype demonstrated consistent assist response during short test runs and
showed clear improvement compared to fully manual pushing.

## What I Learned

- How motor response affects perceived safety
- Why mechanical stability matters as much as electrical power
- How to test assistive systems in small, controlled steps
- How important cable routing and enclosure planning become on moving hardware

## Next Iteration

- Improve torque tuning for heavier loads
- Add better battery and thermal monitoring
- Refine enclosure and cable routing
- Document test results with photos and build notes
