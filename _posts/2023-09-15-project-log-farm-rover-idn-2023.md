## Project Goal

Farm Rover IDN 2023 was my first major project. The goal was to build a small
rover platform that could move through a farm-like environment and support
future agricultural tasks such as inspection, monitoring, or carrying small
tools.

I approached it as a learning project in robotics, mechanical assembly, motor
control, and making hardware behave reliably outside a clean desk setup.

## What I Built

- Rover chassis for rough ground movement
- Motor drive system for mobile control
- Basic control electronics and wiring
- Early testing workflow for movement, turning, and power delivery
- A platform that could be expanded with sensors or task-specific attachments

## Development Notes

Because this was my first project, a lot of the value came from learning how
small design decisions affect the whole machine. Wheel placement, wiring,
battery position, and motor response all changed how stable and useful the
rover felt during testing.

The project gave me a practical introduction to building systems where
mechanical design, electronics, and control logic all have to work together.

## Results

The rover became a working foundation for farm robotics experimentation. More
importantly, it gave me the confidence to keep building physical systems and
documenting the process from idea to prototype.

## What I Learned

- How to think about a robot as a complete system
- Why power delivery and wiring layout matter early
- How much testing changes the original design idea
- How to turn a broad concept into a working prototype

## Next Iteration

- Add stronger documentation with photos and wiring notes
- Improve the chassis for uneven terrain
- Add sensors for field data collection
- Test longer run times with better battery management
