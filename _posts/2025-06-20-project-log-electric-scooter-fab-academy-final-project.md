## Project Goal

The electric scooter was my Fab Academy final project, built during June 2025.
The goal was to create a functional personal mobility prototype that combined
mechanical design, digital fabrication, electronics, and system integration.

This project brought together many Fab Academy skills into one build: designing
parts, fabricating components, assembling hardware, wiring electronics, and
testing the result as a working machine.

## What I Built

- Electric scooter frame and physical structure
- Motor and drive system integration
- Battery and power wiring layout
- Control interface for powered movement
- Fabricated parts made through the Fab Academy workflow
- Final prototype testing and documentation

## Development Notes

The scooter was a bigger integration challenge than a single electronics or
mechanical exercise. Every subsystem depended on the others: the frame had to
hold the load, the electronics had to be protected, the motor had to deliver
usable motion, and the controls had to feel understandable.

Because it was a final project, I treated the build as both a prototype and a
demonstration of the skills I had developed through the course.

## Results

The project resulted in a working electric scooter prototype and a stronger
understanding of how to move from digital design files to a physical machine
that can be assembled, tested, and improved.

## What I Learned

- How to manage a full prototype across multiple disciplines
- How fabrication choices affect assembly and maintenance
- Why power systems need careful layout and protection
- How to document a final project as both a build and a learning record

## Next Iteration

- Add more detailed photos and build diagrams
- Improve enclosure design around the electronics
- Refine cable routing and service access
- Continue testing ride feel, braking, and reliability
